On April 17, Google announced that "Let Google Call" — a feature where AI agents contact local stores on behalf of users to check inventory, pricing, and promotions — is rolling out inside AI Mode in the United States in the coming weeks. The feature launched on Search in November 2025 covering toys, electronics, and health and beauty. Its AI Mode expansion means natural-language queries like "I forgot to pack my prescription sunglasses" can trigger a chain of phone calls from Google to local retailers, with results emailed or texted back. Google's same announcement added individual-hotel price tracking globally for English and Spanish signed-in users, plus travel-trend data: "AI travel assistant" search interest is up 350% year-over-year, and "AI flight booking" is up 315%.

## What the April 17 update actually does

"Let Google Call" triggers when an AI Mode query includes nearby intent. The AI asks clarifying questions, then places multiple calls to local retailers in parallel. Results return by email or text. The November 2025 Search rollout covered toys, electronics, and health and beauty; the AI Mode expansion rolls out in the United States in the coming weeks, per Google's April 17 announcement and TechCrunch's same-day reporting.

Alongside it, Google expanded hotel price tracking from city-level to named-hotel-level for signed-in users globally in English and Spanish. On desktop, users tap a price-tracking toggle after looking up a hotel; on mobile, the option lives under the Prices tab. Email alerts fire when a price changes during chosen dates. Top summer 2026 Google Flights destinations: St. Maarten, Stockholm, Kansas City MO, Sarasota FL.

## Why this is different from what Google already did

Google has shown nearby stores for two decades. What changed on April 17 is that the agent takes the action the user would have taken. The friction that previously limited local commerce queries (calling four stores to find one in-stock item) is gone. For retailers, this turns "product in stock nearby" from an SEO question into an operations question: can Google's agent reach us, and will someone answer?

The SOCi 2026 Local Visibility Index, which we covered on April 18, found AI platforms picking few name-specific local businesses. "Let Google Call" compounds the effect because the AI now has a transactional reason to pick one store from the set. The businesses that survive aren't just the ones AI cites. They are the ones AI can call, whose staff answer.

## The trend data that frames why Google built this now

Search interest in "AI travel assistant" and "AI concierge" is up 350% year-over-year. "AI flight booking" is up 315%. "How to use AI to find flight deals" is trending as a flight-deals query. "Solo travel" is at an all-time high, "women solo travel" at a 15-year high. These are Google's own observed search behaviors from its April 2026 travel trends release, not forecasts. Users search for AI tools more frequently, and Google is shipping supply in response.
